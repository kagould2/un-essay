Effectiveness of the Ban on the Encyclopédie

The ban on the "Encyclopédie" was implemented by the Catholic Church in 1751, shortly after its publication began.

Initially, the ban was effective in limiting the circulation and distribution of the "Encyclopédie," particularly within Catholic-dominated regions and institutions.

However, despite the efforts to suppress it, the "Encyclopédie" continued to be smuggled and circulated clandestinely, reaching a wide audience across Europe.

Over time, the influence of the ban diminished, and the "Encyclopédie" played a significant role in shaping Enlightenment thought and contributing to intellectual and social change.
