Reason for Banning the Encyclopédie

The "Encyclopédie" was banned by the Catholic Church primarily due to its embrace of Enlightenment ideals and its critical examination of established institutions, including the Church itself.

Published in the 18th century, the "Encyclopédie" sought to compile and disseminate knowledge across various fields, promoting rational inquiry, scientific progress, and secular thought.

The Church viewed the content of the "Encyclopédie" as subversive and potentially dangerous, as it challenged traditional authority and advocated for intellectual freedom and skepticism.

As a result, the Catholic Church placed the "Encyclopédie" on the Index of Prohibited Books in 1751, condemning it as heretical and threatening to religious orthodoxy.
