Effectiveness of the Ban on "Discorsi e Dimostrazioni Matematiche"

The ban on Galileo Galilei's "Discorsi e Dimostrazioni Matematiche" was implemented by the Catholic Church shortly after its publication in 1632.

Initially, the ban was effective in suppressing the dissemination of Galileo's ideas, particularly among scholars and within academic institutions under the influence of the Church.

However, despite the efforts to suppress it, the book continued to circulate clandestinely, and its ideas gradually gained acceptance among intellectuals and scientists in subsequent centuries.

By the 19th century, the effectiveness of the ban had diminished significantly, and the Church eventually removed "Discorsi e Dimostrazioni Matematiche" from the Index of Prohibited Books in the mid-19th century, acknowledging its scientific value.
